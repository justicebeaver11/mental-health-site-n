Mental Health

IT'S OK TO BE NOT OK

Mental health is a state of mental well-being that enables people to cope with the stresses of life, realize their abilities, learn well and work well, and contribute to their community

What is Mental Health

Self-care tips

Self-care for mental health can include activities that fit into seven pillars: mental, emotional, physical, environmental, spiritual, recreational, and social. A well-balanced routine should include activities from each pillar. Here are some tips for self-care:
1)Physical health: Exercise regularly, eat healthy meals, stay hydrated, and get enough sleep.
2)Social connections: Prioritize face-to-face time with friends and family, and give to others.
3)Relaxation: Try activities like meditation, yoga, massage, taking a bath, or walking in nature.
4)Learning: Learn new skills or hobbies, or read a book.
5)Mindfulness: Pay attention to the present moment and practice deep breathing.
6)Stress management: Avoid drugs and alcohol, and take breaks from your computer to manage eye strain.
You can also try taking a mental health day when you feel burnt out.

Contagion

Mental health can spread through social processes, such as mimicry, empathy, conformity, social learning, and influence, but it's not contagious like a virus

Emotional Contagion

Emotional contagion is the process by which people "catch" feelings from others. For example, spending time with someone who is consistently anxious or depressed can increase the likelihood of experiencing similar emotions.

Behavioral Contagion

This occurs when behaviors (both positive and negative) spread through groups. For example, if a person in a friend group begins engaging in self-destructive behavior, it might influence others to do the same.

Social Media Influence

Social media can amplify mental health contagion by spreading emotions and behaviors rapidly across networks. Exposure to content related to mental health issues can affect users' mental states.

Mental Health

Symptoms

Mental health symptoms can include a wide range of feelings, behaviors, and changes in physical health:
Emotional Feeling anxious, depressed, or hopeless, having emotional outbursts, or feeling guilty or worthless
Behavioral Withdrawing from friends or activities, engaging in high-risk activities, or having unusual or odd behavior
Physical Changes in appetite or weight, disrupted sleep, aches, headaches, or digestive problems without a clear cause, or feeling especially tired or low in energy
Cognitive Confused thinking, reduced ability to concentrate, or illogical thinking

What Should We Do

There's no sure way to prevent mental illness. However, if you have a mental illness, taking steps to control stress, to increase your resilience and to boost low self-esteem may help keep your symptoms under control.

01

Primary prevention

This focuses on stopping people from developing mental health problems and promoting good mental health for all. It often targets and benefits everyone in a community. Examples include anti-stigma campaigns such as Mental Health Awareness Week or teaching school children about emotions and mental ill health.

02

Secondary prevention

This focuses on supporting people who are more likely to develop mental health problems, either because of characteristics they were born with or experiences they've had. It includes LGBTQIA+ people (because they have a higher chance of being bullied), people who have experienced trauma, people with long-term physical health conditions and victims of hate crimes for example
